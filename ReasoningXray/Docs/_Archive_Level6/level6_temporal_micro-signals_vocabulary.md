🕰️ ReasoningXray Level 6
Temporal Micro-Signals Vocabulary (Patient Experience Layer)

These are very small perception cues that:

normalize tempo

reduce anxiety

signal structural movement

remain clinically believable

never imply prediction

They must feel like gentle temporal orientation, not guidance.

1️⃣ Stabilization Signals
Emotional Risk

Patient feels: stagnation / doubt / “nothing is happening”.

Micro-Signal Set

“Reasoning is being observed over time.”

“This phase often involves quiet monitoring.”

“Consistency is being checked.”

“Less visible change can still be meaningful.”

Tone Requirements

neutral

calm

non-reassuring

observational

2️⃣ Narrowing Signals
Emotional Risk

Patient feels: partial answers / uncertainty fatigue.

Micro-Signal Set

“Possibilities are gradually becoming more focused.”

“Information is helping reduce the field.”

“Direction may become clearer step by step.”

“Progress can feel subtle at this stage.”

Tone Requirements

directional but non-predictive

acknowledges incompleteness

3️⃣ Oscillation Signals
Emotional Risk

Patient feels: confusion / loss of confidence.

Micro-Signal Set

“Reasoning may shift as new information appears.”

“Different explanations can be compared over time.”

“Reconsideration can be part of careful evaluation.”

“Temporary changes do not always mean setback.”

Tone Requirements

normalize fluctuation

protect trust

4️⃣ Consolidation Signals
Emotional Risk

Patient feels: expectation for resolution too early.

Micro-Signal Set

“An explanation may be becoming more structured.”

“Plans can become steadier as reasoning settles.”

“Clarity sometimes increases gradually.”

“Fewer changes can signal growing confidence.”

Tone Requirements

gentle strengthening

avoid finality

5️⃣ Monitoring Plateau Signals
Emotional Risk

Patient feels: abandonment / delay / neglect.

Micro-Signal Set

“Time can help reveal patterns.”

“Monitoring phases can be intentionally quiet.”

“Spacing between visits may be part of observation.”

“Waiting can serve a reasoning purpose.”

Tone Requirements

legitimize stillness

remove guilt or urgency

6️⃣ Acceleration Window Signals
Emotional Risk

Patient feels: overwhelm or sudden urgency.

Micro-Signal Set

“New information can sometimes speed up decisions.”

“Reasoning may move more quickly at certain moments.”

“Several steps can occur closer together.”

“Changes in pace can be expected.”

Tone Requirements

normalize tempo change

prevent alarm

7️⃣ Re-Exploration Loop Signals
Emotional Risk

Patient feels: frustration / “back to start”.

Micro-Signal Set

“Earlier ideas may be revisited when needed.”

“Re-evaluation can occur as understanding grows.”

“Reopening questions can strengthen reasoning.”

“Progress is not always linear.”

Tone Requirements

protect sense of progress

avoid regression framing
