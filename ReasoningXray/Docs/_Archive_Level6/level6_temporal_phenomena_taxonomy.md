🧭 ReasoningXray Level 6
Temporal Phenomena Taxonomy (Scope Definition Layer)

This taxonomy does not describe medical events.
It describes how reasoning feels over time from the patient’s perspective.

It is a perception-structure model, not a diagnostic model.

1. Stabilization Phase
Structural Meaning

Reasoning is no longer expanding widely.
It is holding position to observe consistency.

Experiential Signature

Patients may feel:

“Nothing new is happening”

“Why are we waiting?”

“Are we stuck?”

Actual Reasoning Dynamic

hypothesis is being stress-tested against time

symptoms are being allowed to declare pattern

premature action is being avoided

Temporal Shape

Flat surface with hidden evaluation.

Movement is low-visibility but real.

2. Narrowing Phase
Structural Meaning

Multiple possibilities are gradually being reduced.

Experiential Signature

Patients may feel:

subtle direction without clarity

repeated similar visits

partial answers

Actual Reasoning Dynamic

information density is increasing

reasoning is converging

options are being silently ruled out

Temporal Shape

Gentle funnel.

Progress is directional but incomplete.

3. Oscillation Phase
Structural Meaning

Reasoning temporarily shifts between competing explanations.

Experiential Signature

Patients may feel:

confusion about “changing mind”

sense of instability

concern about uncertainty

Actual Reasoning Dynamic

competing models are being compared

new evidence perturbs prior assumptions

refinement is occurring

Temporal Shape

Controlled back-and-forth movement.

This is not regression.
It is calibration.

4. Consolidation Phase
Structural Meaning

A working explanation is becoming structurally stronger.

Experiential Signature

Patients may feel:

clearer language from doctor

fewer new tests

more specific monitoring

Actual Reasoning Dynamic

reasoning coherence increases

internal contradictions reduce

plan stability increases

Temporal Shape

Slow solidification.

Not dramatic change — but increasing structural confidence.

5. Monitoring Plateau
Structural Meaning

Reasoning intentionally pauses expansion while observing real-world evolution.

Experiential Signature

Patients may feel:

long quiet periods

low contact frequency

perceived inactivity

Actual Reasoning Dynamic

time itself is being used as diagnostic signal

intervention threshold not yet reached

trajectory is still moving

Temporal Shape

Wide calm interval.

This is designed stillness, not neglect.

6. Acceleration Window
Structural Meaning

New decisive information enters the reasoning field.

Experiential Signature

Patients may feel:

sudden activity

multiple decisions close together

fast clarification

Actual Reasoning Dynamic

uncertainty collapses rapidly

branching reduces sharply

reasoning momentum increases

Temporal Shape

Compressed forward movement.

These windows are episodic and unpredictable.

7. Re-Exploration Loop
Structural Meaning

Reasoning temporarily reopens previously reduced possibilities.

Experiential Signature

Patients may feel:

frustration (“we are back to start”)

doubt about prior progress

Actual Reasoning Dynamic

new contradiction detected

earlier assumptions revisited

model reset at a higher information level

Temporal Shape

Loop at a higher altitude.

Not restart.
Re-evaluation.

🧠 Why This Taxonomy Matters

Level 6 will not say:

“You are in Stabilization Phase.”

Instead it will draw micro-signals from these phenomena to:

normalize tempo

legitimize waiting

contextualize revisit patterns

reduce perceived randomness

This gives ReasoningXray:

Temporal literacy.

Patients begin to feel:

Clinical reasoning has seasons, not just steps.
