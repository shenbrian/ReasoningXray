🔒 Micro-Signal Discipline Rules (Critical)
Rule 1 — Shortness

Signals must be:

6–12 words ideally

never paragraph-like

Rule 2 — Non-Deterministic Language

Avoid:

will

soon

likely

expected outcome

Prefer:

may

can

sometimes

often

Rule 3 — Non-Directive

Never:

tell patient what to do

suggest action

suggest reassurance behavior

Rule 4 — Non-Medical Content

Signals must not reference:

tests

treatments

diagnoses

symptoms

Only reasoning tempo.

Rule 5 — Low Frequency

Temporal signals should appear:

only when phenomenon dominance stable

not every visit

not repeated verbatim

🎯 Intended Psychological Effect

Patients begin to feel:

the case has rhythm

uncertainty has structure

quiet periods are meaningful

reasoning can move invisibly

reversals are not failure

tempo shifts are natural

The key transformation:

Waiting becomes interpretable.
